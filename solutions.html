Solutions

It is important to find solutions to gender inequality in job opportunities because it ensures equal access to employment, promotes economic growth, reduces poverty and social exclusion, and fosters a fairer and more just society.

Pay Transparency
By holding employers accountable to set fair pay practices and economically empowering women, pay transparency is a simple tool to close gender and racial wage gaps. For example, in Colorado, where a pay range transparency law has been in effect since 2021, a study found that wages on job postings have gone up on average!. Transparent pay practices provide women with access to salary information, empowering them to negotiate job offers more effectively. With knowledge of the typical salary range for a specific position, women can enter negotiations with confidence and ensure they are being offered fair compensation. This helps prevent situations where women may unknowingly accept lower salaries due to a lack of information.
Social development and cultural openness
It is important to exchange cultures and knowledge in order to abandon intolerance and ossification of customs and traditions. A change in mentality contributes greatly to improving the status of working women, so that negative perceptions and harsh criticisms disappear, thus paving the way for women to work freely. Social development and cultural openness can challenge and transform traditional gender norms and expectations that limit women's career choices. By promoting gender equality and challenging stereotypes, societies can create an environment where women are encouraged to pursue diverse career paths and have equal access to job opportunities
Skill Based Assessments
Employers should be required to pay men and women equally for performing the same job. The government can enforce this through legislation or by providing incentives to employers who demonstrate fair pay practices. Skill-based assessments provide an objective and standardized method for evaluating candidates' abilities and competencies. This approach reduces the influence of biases and subjective judgments that can disadvantage women in traditional hiring processes. By focusing on skills and performance, women can showcase their capabilities and be evaluated solely based on their merit.
Establishing Moral Oversight
Moral oversight ensures the establishment of confidential and accessible reporting mechanisms for workplace issues. This empowers women to report any instances of harassment, discrimination, or unethical behavior without fear of retaliation.
